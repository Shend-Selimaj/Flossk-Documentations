EqualiTECH - Human Rights Hackathon
Project Duration: December 11-13, 2020

Project Description:
EqualiTECH was a regional hackathon designed to harness the technological creativity of young people in the Western Balkans to address injustice and inequality. The event brought together participants from Kosovo, Albania, Macedonia, and Bosnia and Herzegovina, working in teams with mentors to develop innovative solutions. The hackathon promoted collaboration between tech enthusiasts and activists.

Activities:

Opening Remarks: Introductions by Philip Merrell (CRD) and Arianit Dobroshi (FLOSSK).
Keynote Speech: Delivered by Marcin de Kaminski (Human Rights Defenders at Risk).
Team Work: Participants collaborated for seven hours on Saturday, guided by mentors.
Mentorship and Feedback: Continuous support and feedback to refine project ideas.
Final Presentations: Teams presented their projects on Sunday at 17:00.

Beneficiaries:

Participants (ages 18-40) from Kosovo, Albania, Macedonia, and Bosnia and Herzegovina.
Developed new skills and contributed to solutions for addressing social challenges.

Partners:

CRD (Centre for Regional Development)
FLOSSK (Kosovo Free and Open Source Software Association)
IPKO Foundation
YIHR (Youth Initiative for Human Rights)
